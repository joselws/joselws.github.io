Introduction¶

Venezuela is sometimes infamously known for its high inflation, one of the highest in the world. In this document, we will analyze the dollar rates used in Venezuela, identify possible patterns, and elaborate a model that may accurately predict future rates using the Python programming language.

The data¶

The data presented in this analysis are the dollar rates from May 2023 to early July 2023.

There are two mainly used dollar rates in Venezuela:

  • BCV (Banco Central de Venezuela) rate: The official and regulated dollar rate on a given day given by the Government's bank. Companies, registered stores, banks, and any other officially established trademark works under this rate.
  • Dolartoday: Used mostly by unregulated comercial activities and selling goods, sort of the "black market" rate. Since legal and unlimited exchange houses don't exist within the country, dollar bills are usually bought under this rate as well. Taxis person-to-person services, and homemade goods mostly work under this rate.

Model¶

Our goal is then build regression models we can use to predict future values of bcv and dolartoday for a given day. In this regard, we are going to need two separate models, one for predicting bcv rates and another for predicting dolartoday rates. In both models, the only feature variable that will be used in this project is day.

From the previous graph, we can see that bcv data very closely resembles a line, so it's a perfect candidate for a linear model. Therefore, for this model, we will use single-variable linear regression.

Dolartoday's data highly resembles a function of $f(x) = sin(g(x)) + h(x)$, where both $g$ and $h$ are linear functions. Although we can try to fit it within a linear or polynomial model, we decide that, due to its high complexity, this model will be left out of the document analysis for time being, and will be included in future updates of this project.

Conclusion¶

We found out bcv rates increase over time in a pseudo linear fashion, this allowed us to take advantage of it and build an extraordinary linear model to make predictions of its value with a given day as input. We evaluated its performance and the results were amazing.

The resulting linear model follow this function: $f(x) = 1.03x + 26.37$.

Thanks to this model, we were able to predict the upcoming bcv rates for August.

dolartoday rates are more erratic and it's harder to fit this variable in the best possible model. Although a linear model could have the done the job pretty much fine, we decided to leave it for later updates.

Extra¶

Although we didn't work on a predictive model for dolartoday data, we can take advantage of the fact that dolartoday tends to be above a certain level of bcv, so if we can figure out what this level is, whether it is a raw value or a percent increase, the relationship between dolartoday and bcv values can be established and can be used to have accurate predictions for dolartoday based on its corresponding bcv value. For example, we may infer from our data that a dolartoday value is around $\$1.5$ higher than its bcv counterpart, and its value can be predicted from it.

Rate difference in dollars¶

Our first strategy will be to find the difference between dolartoday and bcv and study the distribution of this data through a kernel density estimate (KDE) plot.

Mean of this dollar difference: 1.3471641791044773
Standard distribution of this difference: 0.36043242534888065

Interestingly, the distribution of this difference between the rates follows the pattern of a normal distribution with mean around $\$1.35$ and standard deviation of $\$0.36$. So, statistically speaking, it's $68\%$ likely that the dolartoday of its bcv counterpart would be within one (1) standard deviations away from the mean, $95\%$ likely it will be two (2) standard deviations away from the mean, and so on. For example, if a bcv value for a day is $\$28.00$, then it's $68\%$ likely the dolartoday rate for that day will be in the range of $\$28.99$ and $\$29.71$.

To make sure these results are unbiased, we will plot this difference over the days.

Although erratic, there seems to be an increasing for this difference. If so, then our conclusions made early won't hold much ground long term, unless this method is re-calculated every so often to avoid inaccurate results. Still, it was interesting to find this pattern and relationship between the two variables, and to find out that their difference falls in a normal distribution.

Another strategy we could've followed is to compute the difference not as a raw number, but as a ratio between dolartoday and bcv. The results wouldn't have been much different, however.
